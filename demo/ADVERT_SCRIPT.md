## Voiceover script (clean read — ~430 words, ≈2:55 at a calm pace)

> Eighty-four percent of students study by re-reading. Only eleven percent ever test themselves. We use the least effective method — the most.
>
> Now imagine you’re one of the ten percent who are dyslexic. Or one of the hundreds of thousands of students still waiting, right now, for an ADHD assessment. Standard study material was never built for you.
>
> What if your homework could meet you where you are?
>
> Meet Adapt. One choice — and everything changes. Fonts, colour, spacing, pacing, even how our AI talks to you.
>
> Paste anything, and Adapt re-chunks it into plain, readable pieces — keeping every fact. Still not clicking? Get it as an analogy, a story, or step by step.
>
> But here’s what nobody else does. Every other app explains things *to* you. Adapt flips it — *you* teach it. Meet Gem: a classmate who knows nothing yet. You explain a concept in your own words… and Gem finds exactly what you missed.
>
> Watch. We’re teaching Gem photosynthesis — but we never mention chlorophyll. Hit the button… and there it is. Adapt didn’t just mark us wrong. It found the one idea we skipped, and asked the exact question a real tutor would.
>
> And we didn’t invent this. Researchers have proven it since the nineteen-nineties: actually teaching something more than doubles how much you keep, versus just studying it. Adapt turns that into a tool any student can use — alone, on any topic, at two a.m.
>
> Under the hood, Google’s Gemini returns a strict, structured gap analysis: a score, your strengths, the exact gaps, and the next question to ask. Gemini is the reasoning engine — the accessibility engineering is ours.
>
> Adapt. Built for every mind.

## Exact screen-capture shot list (record these clips — real app, real data)

Record at **1280×800**, the app in a clean Chrome window (or the desktop assistant `--full`). Use the **cached demo path** so it’s instant and identical every take (the fallbacks hold real Gemini responses).

- **CLIP A — Profile morph (record ~12s):** onboarding → click **ADHD** (watch it go dark) → sidebar chip **Dyslexia** (watch it warm to ivory) → **Classic**. Slow, deliberate clicks.
- **CLIP B — Reader (~12s):** Reader tab → paste the photosynthesis paragraph → **Make it readable** → let chunks render → toggle **Dys font**, then **Contrast**.
- **CLIP C — Explain (~10s):** Explain tab → paste a concept → click **Analogy**, then **Story**, then **Step-by-step**, letting each render.
- **CLIP D — Teach-it-Back HERO (~40s, the star):** Teach tab → topic “Photosynthesis” → **Start** → type turn 1 (omit chlorophyll) → wait for Gem → type turn 2 → **See what we mapped out** → let the ring animate → **tap the chlorophyll gap card** to reveal Gem’s hint → scroll the “version worth keeping”.
- **CLIP E — Structured JSON glimpse (~5s, optional):** the browser devtools Network tab showing the `/api/finalize` JSON response, or just a slow pan over the gap report. Sells “real engineering, not a wrapper”.

**Turn 1 to type (verbatim):** *“Photosynthesis is how plants make their own food. They take water from the soil and carbon dioxide from the air, and use sunlight to turn them into glucose and oxygen.”*
**Turn 2 to type (verbatim):** *“The glucose is the plant’s food, and the oxygen gets released into the air through the leaves.”*
(Chlorophyll is never typed → the gap report is guaranteed to surface it.)

1. Record **CLIPS A–D** (skip E). ~5 min.
2. Skip the mic. The **big text overlays** in the table above carry the message as captions — punchy, silent-friendly (most people watch demos muted anyway).
3. Hand me the clip filenames + a music file.
