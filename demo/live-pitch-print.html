Adapt — Live Pitch Script

3 minutes · spoken on stage with the real app · LeafHacks ’26
If a judge only hears one line: “Every study app explains things to you — Adapt flips it: you teach the AI, and it finds the exact thing you didn’t know you missed.”

Before you speak — 30-second setup

  • App open on http://localhost:3000 in Chrome, full screen, profile not yet picked.
  • The photosynthesis paragraph already pasted in the Reader (so you can flip fast).
  • Wifi on — but it doesn’t matter: cached fallbacks mean the demo works even if the internet dies.
  • Phone ready with the QR code to hand a judge.

① Hook 0:00–0:20

SAY — “Quick question: how do you revise? …Yeah. You read it again. Everyone does. 84% of students re-read, and only 11% ever test themselves — and re-reading barely works. Now imagine you’re dyslexic, like 1 in 10 people. Or one of the half a million people in England waiting right now for an ADHD assessment. That wall of text was never built for you.”

DO — Nothing yet. Look at them, not the screen. Let the 549,000 land.

② The flip 0:20–0:40

SAY — “So we built Adapt. One big idea: every other study tool explains things to you. Adapt makes you teach it. But first — it meets you where you are.”

DO — Click ADHD Focus. Pause as the whole app re-themes.

SAY — “One click, and the entire app changes — font, colour, spacing, and quietly, how our AI talks to you. That’s not a filter; it’s one profile object driving the interface and the AI’s prompt. That part’s our engineering.”

DO — Quick click Dyslexia to show it warm to ivory + Lexend, then carry on.

③ Reader 0:40–1:00

SAY — “First, reading. Paste anything dense…”

DO — Reader → paste the paragraph → Make it readable.

SAY — “…and every fact is kept — just re-chunked into something that breathes. Gemini does the reasoning; we shape it for the profile. Dyslexia font, high-contrast, the lot.”

DO — Tap Dys font or Contrast once, then move on. Don’t linger.

④ The hero — Teach it Back 1:00–2:10 · slow down

SAY — “But here’s the one that wins the room. Meet Gem — a classmate who knows absolutely nothing. I’m going to teach it photosynthesis. Watch what I leave out.”

DO — Teach it back → topic Photosynthesis → Start → type turn 1:

“Photosynthesis is how plants make their own food — they take in sunlight, water and carbon dioxide, and use the energy from the sun to make food and give off oxygen.”

DO — Send. Gem replies with a question — read it aloud to the judges.

SAY — “See — it doesn’t lecture. It asks. One sharp question. So I answer, a bit vaguely…”

DO — Type turn 2 (the fuzzy one, on purpose):

“Umm… the green stuff in the leaves? The bit that makes them look green?”

DO — Send → See what we mapped out → let the score ring animate.

SAY — “And there it is. I never once said the word chlorophyll — so it found the exact gap I didn’t know I’d left. With a hint, a follow-up, and a cleaner rewrite. Re-reading would never have caught that. Teaching it did — in about ten seconds.”

DO — Tap the chlorophyll gap card. Hand a judge the QR code: “Scan that — try it on your own phone while I finish.”

⑤ Science + tech 2:10–2:40

SAY — “And this isn’t a gimmick. Teaching something is one of the most evidence-backed ways to learn — actually teaching it more than doubles what you keep versus just studying it. Researchers proved that since the 1990s; we built the tool that lets any student do it, alone, at 2am. Under the hood, Gemini returns a strict, schema-enforced JSON gap analysis — not begged from a prompt, enforced. Gemini is the reasoning engine — the accessibility engineering is ours.”

⑥ Close 2:40–3:00

SAY — “Adapt. You don’t read it again — you teach it once. Built for every mind, especially the ones that learn differently. …and yeah. It was chlorophyll.” (smile — done)

If the live demo breaks — keep smiling

API / wifi dies: keep clicking — “everything you’re seeing runs on cached responses right now, so it never breaks on stage.” (It genuinely does — you’re telling the truth.)

Voice mic won’t start: “voice is a bonus — I’ll just type it,” and type. Never fight the mic on stage.

Totally frozen: talk over the still screen — you know the script; the words carry it. Refresh only as a last resort.

Q&A — fast, confident answers

  • “Isn’t this just a Gemini wrapper?” — “No — the profile object drives both the UI and the prompt, and the gap analysis is a two-phase pipeline against a strict schema. Gemini reasons; the accessibility system is ours.”
  • “Why not sign language?” — “Full signing avatars are an unsolved research problem — we scoped honestly to what we could ship brilliantly.”
  • “Aren’t learning styles debunked?” — “Yes — that’s why we call them explanation modes, not learning styles.”
  • “What’s next?” — “Dyscalculia support, live voice tutoring, and a spelling-recognition mode.”
  • “Does the font actually help dyslexia?” — “Evidence is mixed, so we don’t claim a cure — we give the choice. That’s the accessible thing to do.”

Delivery notes

  • Speak to the judges, glance at the screen. The app is your slide, not your script.
  • Rehearse the chlorophyll beat twice — the pause before Gem’s question is where the room leans in.
  • Hand over the QR during the demo — judges trying it themselves = instant Usefulness + UX points.
  • ~450 spoken words ≈ 3:00 at a calm pace. Running long? Cut the Reader beat (③) — never Teach-it-Back.

30-second elevator backup

“Adapt is a study app for people who learn differently. It re-themes to your brain — dyslexia, ADHD — then does what no one else does: instead of explaining to you, it makes you teach a curious AI, and it finds the exact thing you got wrong. Teaching is one of the most proven ways to learn, and we made it something any student can do alone. Built on Gemini — the accessibility engineering is ours.”
